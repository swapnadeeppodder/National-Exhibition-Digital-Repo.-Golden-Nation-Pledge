About the Team

Dipan Bandyopadhyay provided the original concept, vision, and oversight. As mentor, he guided the project’s scope, ensured academic alignment, and helped the team maintain a professional standard throughout the development cycle.

Debargha Sarkar was responsible for the architectural foundation of the platform. He developed the core backbone, block pages, cultural gallery, and the initial version of the chatbot, integrating design and functionality into a cohesive ecosystem.

Koushik Dutta enhanced the chatbot by extending Debargha’s base design, implementing smarter response handling and modernizing its interaction flow. He also contributed meaningful refinements to improve the overall usability of the website.

Anulekha Koley managed the Science Project showcase section. She structured content for clarity, ensuring projects were represented in an accessible yet professional way, reflecting the exhibition’s educational purpose.

Swapnadeep Podder contributed to front-end development by enhancing layout structure, responsiveness, and design consistency, ensuring a polished, accessible, and user-friendly interface that improves overall usability.

Soumili Kayal handled front-end refinements, improving layout consistency and enhancing visual polish. Her contributions ensured the site remained responsive and intuitive across devices.

Soumya Chakraborty led backend development, creating robust server-side functionality to handle content organization, data flow, and smooth system operation.

Titash Majumder applied interface touch-ups and fine-grained adjustments, ensuring that small details—from alignment to typography—contributed to a seamless presentation.

Sagnika Sinha contributed accurate stall information, centralizing data for the exhibition map and ensuring visitors could navigate effortlessly.

Together, this diverse team—under the guidance of Dipan Bandyopadhyay—transformed an idea into a functional, professional exhibition platform that merges design, technology, and information management.
